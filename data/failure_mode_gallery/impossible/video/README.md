# Failure Mode Definition

## F1: Physical Violation
The generated video depicts interactions that violate basic physical principles. Objects may behave in ways that contradict real-world physics, such as applying unrealistic force, producing impossible material responses, or interacting without proper contact or support. Even if the scene appears visually coherent, the interaction itself cannot realistically occur under normal physical constraints.

## F2: Temporal Inconsistency
The generated video fails to maintain temporal consistency across frames. Objects may abruptly change position, shape, or state without a continuous motion trajectory. These discontinuities break the progression of the interaction and prevent the video from depicting a stable sequence of cause-and-effect events.

## F3: Interaction Misexecution
The generated video fails to correctly execute the intended interaction described in the prompt. While the relevant objects may appear in the scene, the action is performed incorrectly, incompletely, or in an unintended manner. As a result, the interaction does not lead to the expected outcome or state change.

# Examples

## f1 (Descriptive). 
The walnut is crushed instantly the moment the glass jar is placed over it. This defies physical constraints as no significant downward force or impact was applied; the jar merely touches the shell, and it shatters as if under a hydraulic press.

## f1 (Predictive). 
A whole, uncut lemon is squeezed with a soft sponge, causing a massive amount of juice to pour out. This is a logical error because a lemon must be sliced to release juice in that manner, and a soft sponge would not provide the structural pressure needed to crush a whole fruit.

## f2 (Descriptive).
The paper dynamics are completely inconsistent with reality. As the paper is slid under the door, it instantly "teleports" or transforms into a crumpled ball on the other side. There is no continuous motion or physical reason for the paper to crumple itself while passing through a flat gap.

## f2 (Predictive).
This video features a major object permanence error. The person starts by reaching for ice cubes pinned to a corkboard, but as they interact with them, the ice cubes instantly morph into turquoise paper clips. The AI failed to maintain the identity of the objects during the action.

## f3 (Descriptive).
The video shows a feather duster being pressed against a lemon, which then starts leaking juice into a glass. This represents a contextual hallucination where the AI confuses the texture of a feather duster with a squeezing tool, and again, ignores the fact that the lemon is uncut.

## f3 (Predictive).
The expected action—using a heavy water bottle to smash a car window—is replaced by a nonsensical physics glitch. Instead of an impact, the water from inside the bottle appears to "melt" through the glass or splash against it in a way that causes the glass to ripple and shatter like liquid, rather than breaking from kinetic force.
